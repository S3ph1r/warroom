Explanatory notes to the earnings statement 1. Structure of the earnings statementThe earnings statement contains detailed information on the income from capital assets generated in the calendar year 2024. It serves to provide explanations for the annual tax certificate. You can refer to the individual income items listed in the earnings statement to understand the composition of the amounts in the respective totals lines of the tax certificate.The earnings statement is always divided into two sections: The first section is referred to as the summary statement, which shows the overall totals of the individual income earnings and capital gains. The second section shows the individual income earnings and capital gains. The list is broken down by income group.Please note that the tax withheld does not have a settling effect for business investors; it merely constitutes an advance payment on the resulting tax liability. Income earnings and capital gains from investments held as business assets generally constitute business income, which is subject to income tax or corporation tax and, if applicable, trade tax.Non-resident taxpayers receive the earnings statement for information purposes only. The earnings statement has been prepared in accordance with German law. As a rule, taxpayers with limited tax liability, i.e. clients who are resident or ordinarily resident abroad for tax purposes, are subject to German withholding tax on their domestic dividends, interest from domestic convertible bonds and income from domestic participation rights similar to shares and annuities. In most cases, all other income earnings and capital gains are exempt from the deduction of German withholding tax.2. Notes on special circumstancesa)Loss offsetting / crediting of foreign withholding taxFor income earnings and capital gains from accounts and securities accounts managed for business purposes, there is no offsetting of losses and no crediting of foreign withholding tax.b)Order of use (first-in-first-out)Please note that the capital gains or losses from the sale or redemption of securities have been determined according to the "first-in-first-out" order of use.c)Disclosure of capital gainsFor business investors, gains from the sale or redemption of securities as well as from futures transactions within the meaning of Section 20 (2) of the German Income Tax Act (EStG) are subject to German withholding tax only in the case of investors who are not corporations and who have not submitted an exemption certificate to Baader Bank within the meaning of Section 43 (2) of the German Income Tax Act (EStG).d)Income from investment fundsUntil 31┬áDecember 2017, income from investment funds was generally subject to the same taxation as income from direct investments (transparency principle). The taxation system for income from investment funds has changed fundamentally since 1┬áJanuary 2018 due to the application of the German Investment Tax Reform Act (InvStRefG).As a result of the German Investment Tax Reform Act (InvStRefG), it is no longer necessary to distinguish between domestic and foreign investment funds.Units in investment funds are deemed to have been notionally sold at the close of 31┬áDecember 2017 and newly acquired on 1┬áJanuary 2018 as a result of the German Investment Tax Reform Act (InvStRefG). As a rule, the redemption price as at 31┬áDecember 2017 was used as the notional sale and acquisition price of the units as at 1┬áJanuary 2018. The notional sale on 31┬áDecember 2017 is taxed at the time of the actual sale of the units in the Fund. These units are referred to as "old units".

The partial exemption rate depends on the type of fund. For investment funds classified as equity funds, 30┬ápercent of the investment income is exempt from taxation. For accumulating and partially accumulating funds, the income equivalent to distributions was replaced by what is known as the "preliminary lump sum". The preliminary lump sum is levied after the end of each calendar year if the investment fund has achieved capital gains during the calendar year and has distributed nothing or only a small proportion thereof. In addition, the general market interest rate level is relevant when calculating the preliminary lump sum. Beginning of the year 2024 a preliminary lump sum was calculated for the previous year. If the investment fund was acquired in the calendar year in which the preliminary lump sum was reported (i.e. in the previous year), then the preliminary lump sum is reduced by 1/12 for each full month prior to the date of purchase. For tax purposes, the preliminary lump sum only accrues at the beginning of the following year. If units in investment funds that fall under the application of the German Investment Tax Reform Act (InvStRefG) as of 1 January 2018 are sold or redeemed, the proceeds from the sale are calculated less the acquisition costs and the accumulated preliminary lump sums already taxed, and taking into account the partial exemption.Please note that the earnings statement does not disclose a share gain/loss on sales of investment funds for the proportionate period of ownership.  f)Foreign exchange transactionsForeign exchange transactions include an obligation to buy or sell a specific amount of foreign currency at a future date or during a specific period of time at a rate determined when the transaction was entered into. The tax liability pursuant to Section 20 (2) sentence 1 no. 3a of the German Income Tax Act (EStG) includes forward exchange transactions aimed at achieving difference equalisation. According to the German Federal Ministry of Finance (BMF) circular dated 19┬áMay 2022 (marginal note 38), foreign exchange transactions aimed at achieving difference equalisation are deemed to be income from capital assets and are therefore in principle subject to German withholding tax. However, Baader Bank is not able to identify the transactions accordingly. For this reason, German withholding tax has, in general, not been withheld on foreign exchange transactions. In addition, currency gains/losses from the sale or repayment of a securitised or non-securitised interest-bearing capital claim or an interest-bearing foreign currency credit balance (e.g. interest-bearing foreign currency account) are to be considered as income from capital assets pursuant to Section 20 (2) sentence 1 no. 7 and (4) sentence 1 of the German Income Tax Act (EStG). This new regulation was included in the German Federal Ministry of Finance (BMF) circular dated 19┬áMay 2022, marginal note 131, and, through the publication of the BMF circular, already applies at the tax assessment level.
